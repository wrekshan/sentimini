Aims

By default, you are signed up to participate in the Open Data Project, which has three goals:

Improving user experience

These are questions related to what you, as a person who uses this tool, really want.
  • What is the optimal frequency of text messages?
  • What do people use the text messages for?
  • When are the texts answered and when are they not?

    • Validity of method

    These are questions related to determining if it is even sensible to measure states of being in this way (mainly statistical in nature)
  • Do these measurements actually relate to something in real life?
  • What are the minimum number of observations needed to draw a conclusion from quantitative comparisons?
  • Do instructions alter responses?
  • Does learning or behavior change occur in relationship to behavior with Sentimini?

    • Understanding human experience

    This is the big question. There are 9 billion people on this planet, each one with a very rich, but very different experience than my own. Through the internet and mobile technology, we are literally more connected than ever.

    The number of questions here seem endless:
  • What are the different emotions people experience throughout the day?
  • What are the circadian variation in human experience?
  • How does experience change with age? with geographic location?
  • What encourages human flourishing?

    • Why Open Data?

    Here are a collection of facts to set the stage:
  • XXXX texts are sent per day from XXX text enabled cell phone
  • XX billion people have the ability to text (vs XX billion with a smart phone)
  • Latern start up for XX million, Pacifica start up
  • Nature infographic
  • XX million people already seeking help from mobile device apps

    • The basic premise of Sentimini, texting people questions or content and collecting their responses, is decpetively simple. In reality, this or similiar methodology, is already being used by doctors, psychologists, and researchers are already this or similiar methodology to diagnosis people.

    Nature has published a great essay and inforgraphic making the case for the need for both the development and evaluation of using technology for wellness (read it here).

    It would be great for an open data framework to exist so that we can learn.

    How

    By default, you are signed up to participate in the Open Data Project. If you do not want to participate, you can uncheck the box in the Settings page.

    Sentimini will send you questions in blocks of three, one right after the other.

    You will be sent around 1 block of texts per week, taking less than 1 minute.

    Just think about the question for a moment and answer. If you do not want to answer, you do not have to answer. If you do not like a question in the block of texts, you can just text, "next", to receive the next question.

    An example block could be:
  • What is your age?
  • How sleepy are you (0-10)?
  • How much goodness is in your experience (0-10)?

    • or:
  • How happy are you in life (0-10)?
  • How satisified are you in life (0-10)?
  • What is happiness (please write a definition in a few sentences)?

    • Your participation is needed to answer these questions. But, a goal is to ask interesting and enriching questions.

    Questions will be asked semi-randomly focusing on specific research questions. Research questions, plans, data, and results are shared on the blog. If you have a research question you would like to ask, please contact me here.
