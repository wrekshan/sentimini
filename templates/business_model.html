Overview

This is a rough draft of a feasiblity study

Sentimini is designed to:
  • Contact people at semi-random times
  • Collects and analyzes the responses

    • Ideally, this will be done in:
  • A self-supporting manner through either donations, a paid consumer feature, facilitating communication on wellness settings, or providing tools for people to communicate with large groups of people.
  • A open data / open source / open science framework.

    • Is this feasible? As far as I can tell, there are three forms of feasibility.
  • Technical/Methodological - can this be done with current technology?
  • Social - will people hear about this site? Will they use it?
  • Financial - how much will such a system cost?

    • Technical Feasibility

    There is a precedent:
  • A large number of companies are using automatically generated text messages to communicate with their clients (TinyPulse, Uber, Lyft, etc).
  • In addition, text messaging has been used in numerous research projects (mappiness, havard guy, kaheleme, UCB, etc).
  • Other apps use text messaging (good habit maker, food thing christine talks about)

    • The trickiest part of this is actually sending the text messages. This can be done in three ways.

    SMS Gateways

  • Free
  • May not be reliable
  • Takes effort to set up

    • Using Carrier Emails

  • Free
  • Reliability isn't great
  • Very easy to set up
  • This is the default experience for Sentimini

    • Paid Services

  • Cost money
  • Very easy to set up
  • Very reliable
  • Twilio, Plivio, etc

    • Therefore, Sentimini has demonstrated technical feasibility through precendent and working product.

    Social Feasibility

    The best way to determine if people would use Sentimini is to estimate the range of people using similiar services. Google's play store offers rough number of downloads per apps.

    Mindfulness apps:

  • Headspace: 1 - 5 million
  • The Mindfulness App: 50k-100k
  • Mindfulness: Art Of Being: 50k-100k

    • Emotion tracking apps:

  • Mood panda: 50k-100k
  • t2 mood tracker: 100k-500k
  • In Flow - Mood Diary: 10k-50k
  • Emotion Sense (Cambridge): 10k-50k
  • Moodlytics: 50k-100k
  • Diary - Mood Tracker: 100k-500k

    • Psychology/Wellness apps:

  • Pacifica: 100k-500k
  • Happify: 100k-500k
  • Depression CBT Self-Help Guide: 100k - 500k

    • Therefore,a conservative estimate of the expected numbers of users would 10k-50k, assuming that Sentimini is moderately operational. The actual number of users could be higher because Sentimini:
  • Uses technology that is ubiquitious (My grandmother had a text enabled cell phone 10 years ago!)
  • Is the simpliest form of the technology and would be useful in many different areas

    • A word of mouth marketing strategy is also feasible.
  • Sentimini and the open data project are interesting and will produce content that increases SEO
  • There is a lot of interest in using technology for wellness
  • Many blogs cover products like Sentimini

    • Therefore, Sentimini has demonstrated social feasibility through precendent.

    Assumptions:

    This assumes a freeium business model, with two tiers:
  • Free - Sends texts of varying content at semi-random times and collects responses
  • Paid - Additionally, sends texts of specific content at specific times and collects responses

    • This is a useful price point because:
  • This is an advanced feature - If you are looking for a system to text you specific content at specific times, you would be willing to pay for it
  • It is probable that the free methods of sending texts will be too unreliable to truly provide the ability to send specific texts at specific times.
  • Therefore, it would cost money to provide this service (via Twilio or Plivo)

    • This model is optimistic in that is assumes a free version can be provided (which is likely). However, if this is not the case, possible products could be:
  • Facilitating communication in wellness settings
  • Offering specific experiences (quit smoking now, record your dreams, pain evaluation, etc.)
