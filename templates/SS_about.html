You can change your life!
Technology should serve life

One the biggest problems I faced in practicing mindfulness and trying to improve my life is that I would just forget. I don't really need technology to tell me what to do....I already know what to do. I just need a little bit of help to remember.

Sentimini is designed to help

Sentimini is designed sent texts at semi-random times. It has been so beneficial to me to be lost in anger or worry only to receive a text reminding me to "Be here now!". Even if I continue to be angry, for one moment, I had the chance to wake up.

Random sampling is a great way to quantitatively learn about experience.
Randomness is beautiful

Sentimini sends texts at semi-random times. If you sign up, you will receive a text at some semi-random time in the future from yourself from the past. It may arrive at just the right time, with mystery and surpise, helping you acheive your goals.

More than this, if you want to gain valid quanitative conclusions about your life, then random sampling is necessary to avoid bias. You know that you aren't just collecting data when you are in a particular state of mind, so you can trust Sentimini to help you learn if you experience more sadness than happiness or if a medication is working for pain or depression.

Texts are for everybody

Sentimini sends texts to learn about and improve the human experience. It isn't an iOS app, something you have to buy, or a class. This isn't a mistake. Texts are inexpensive and take little time. Texting is a part of life and nearly everyone can do it.

So the first goal is to provide everyone, from the wealthy to the poor and from the old to the young, simple tools to learn about and transforms their lives. The second goal is to develop an open dataset on lived human experience so that we may learn about others, replicate research findings in a citizen science framework, and use data to improve health and mental health care.

Who made this?

It is sometimes strange to see a website made by an unknown person, so I wanted to tell you a bit about myself. My name is William Rekshan. I made Sentimini because I took a class on meditation and emotions, but I kept on forgetting to pay attention! I looked around for an emotion tracker that would help me and I found none that were quite right.
So I made Sentimini. I've personally used it for over a year. I've found it truly amazing to be so caught up in my throughts only to receive a text encouraging me to just breathe, or that I will die, or that I actually do love the people in my life.
I've worked in psychology research for nearly 10 years! Please see my LinkedIn or published research papers for an idea of who I am. I've learned so much about statistics, this world, and what it means to be human through this work. I just wanted to give back!

Why was this made?

Sentimini was originally intended to be an emotion tracking app. When I looked for emotion tracking apps, I never found really solved my problem....that I just kept on forgetting to pay attention to my emotions. I really needed an app that would remind me at random times throughout the day.
I've worked in neuroscience and psychology research for almost 10 years, with 4 of those as a statistician, so I knew immediately that this is a wonderful, less biased way of quantitatively exploring lived human experience. Texts have been used in health care and research. Nobel wining economist, Daniel Kahneman uses experience sampling to learn about lived experience, researchers at the London School of Economics and Havard have used texts to learn about happiness, non-profits CareMessage and Crisis Text Line use texts for health care. At least 153 clincial trials have utilized SMS in some way and 480 have evaluated mindfulness (accessed through enigma.io). While working to develop fMRI based neurofeedback to assist people experiencing chronic pain, I witnessed people transform their lives by being curious about their experiences (in this case mindfullness while experience pain).
I wanted to be able to learn about others through research and offer tools to improve life that I would use myself. So, I made Sentimini for myself. I've used it to improve my own life. I know what I want to do in life. I know what I need to do to make myself happier. I just need a small nudge to remember. I hope that others will find it helpful. I hope to be able to do open-source citizen science with it.
Please email me at william@sentimini.com if you have any questions or thoughts! I'd love to speak with you! I hope you enjoy!
